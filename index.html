01 —— The Moment

Knowledge is locked behind the wrong doors.

For four hundred years, the scientific paper has been the unit of human knowledge. It was a printing-press artifact — rigid, slow, gatekept. We kept the format. We kept the gatekeepers. We now live inside machines that can read and reason across the entire corpus in seconds.

The format has not caught up. The gatekeeping has.

02 —— The Problem

The corpus we built our civilization on is broken.

Paywalled

Most of humanity cannot read the research humanity funded.

Gatekept

If you lack a PhD, an affiliation, or a grant, your ideas have no door.

Format-stuck

PDFs optimised for 1665 printing presses. Not for humans. Not for models.

Self-censored

Rules about what can be said have quietly become rules about what can be thought.

03 —— Insight

The printing press ended the first dark age. We need the next press.

Gutenberg did not invent literacy. He removed the friction between the mind and the page. The result was the Renaissance.

Today's friction is no longer mechanical. It is institutional. Journals, reviewers, impact factors, and institutional affiliation have become the moats around knowledge. AI has made the old format obsolete without giving us a new one.

Knowledge wants to flow. The rails that slow it down are now optional.

04 —— What it is

CodexVault is an open archive of codexes.

A codex is what a paper becomes when you strip away the gatekeepers.

  • Authored by anyone. No affiliation, no permission.
  • Free to read, forever. No paywall, no tier, no login wall.
  • AI-native. Structured, versioned, machine-consumable by default.
  • Unruled. No committee decides what knowledge is worth keeping.

06 —— Principles

Four lines we do not cross.

  1. Free for all.

    Readers pay nothing. Ever. No tier will exist above this line.

  2. Open to publish.

    No affiliation, no permission, no editor. The archive is an act of writing, not of being chosen.

  3. Permanent by design.

    A codex published is a codex kept. Content-addressed, redundantly stored, beyond any single authority's power to erase.

  4. No rules — responsibility instead.

    We do not curate truth. We publish provenance, context, and the tools to reason. The reader adjudicates, not us.

07 —— How it works

From draft to permanent archive in one flow.

  1. 01

    Write

    Author a codex in structured markdown. Prose, code, data, proofs — first-class.

  2. 02

    Sign

    Cryptographic identity. No institution required. The signature is the credential.

  3. 03

    Publish

    Content-addressed, versioned, replicated. The codex exists everywhere CodexVault reaches.

  4. 04

    Evolve

    Forks, new versions, diffs. Challenges attach as codexes of their own.

The archive grows. Nothing is retracted — only forked, challenged, or superseded. History stays legible.
